The Method

Why interviews, not questionnaires

Most AI assessments ask you to fill in a form or score yourself against a readiness framework. The problem is obvious: how would you know what you don't know? If you could already identify your AI opportunities, you wouldn't need an audit.

The Insider Method works differently. We have structured conversations with the people who actually do the work — not just the owner, but the team. People describe their day. They mention the things that take too long, the manual steps they've accepted as normal, the information that never quite reaches the right person at the right time. They surface opportunities they don't even recognise as opportunities.

That's where the real value is. Not in a generic framework — in the specific, messy, human reality of how your business actually operates.

Your Data

How we handle your information

We take data seriously. Interviews are recorded with consent and transcribed using AI. Transcripts are stored securely and used only for analysis during the audit. They are not shared with third parties. After the report is delivered, you can request deletion of all interview data at any time.

The audit report itself is yours. We don't retain copies beyond what's needed for quality assurance and our own records, and we'll never share your business information without your explicit permission.

Common questions

"We're too small for this kind of thing"

The audit is designed specifically for businesses with 10-150 people. This is the size where AI makes the biggest difference — and where the big consultancies don't bother looking. If anything, you're exactly the right size.

"We're already using some AI tools"

Good — that means you're not starting from zero. The audit will tell you whether you're using the right tools in the right places, and surface opportunities you haven't found yet. Tools without a map is the problem the audit solves.

"What do we actually get at the end?"

A written report with every AI and automation opportunity in your business, plotted on an impact-vs-effort matrix. Plus a 45-minute walkthrough call to make sure it's clear and actionable. It's a deliverable you can hand to your team, your board, or a developer and say: "This is the plan."

"I'd need to involve my team — is that disruptive?"

Each person gives 30 minutes of their time for a conversation about how they work. That's it. No workshops, no away days, no homework. And the interview approach actually brings the team in — it's collaborative, not top-down.

"Can't I just figure this out myself?"

You could — and we're building a free mini self-audit tool to help you do exactly that (coming soon). The full audit goes deeper: independent interviews with your team surface things that don't come up when the owner is in the room, and the AI-assisted analysis catches patterns across conversations that individual self-assessment misses.
