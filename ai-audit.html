The Method

Why interviews, not questionnaires

Most AI assessments ask you to fill in a form or score yourself against a readiness framework. The problem is obvious: how would you know what you don't know? If you could already identify your AI opportunities, you wouldn't need an audit.

The Insider Method works differently. We have structured conversations with the people who actually do the work. People describe their day. They mention the things that take too long, the manual steps they've accepted as normal, the information that never quite reaches the right person at the right time. They surface opportunities they don't even recognise as opportunities.

That's where the real value is. Not in a generic framework. In the specific, messy, human reality of how your business actually operates.

Your Data

We never touch your systems

The entire audit is built from conversations and publicly available research. We never access your internal systems, databases, software, or client data. No logins. No file access. No data extraction. Everything we learn comes from what your team tells us.

Interviews are recorded with your consent and transcribed for analysis. If you'd rather use your own recording method, or provide notes instead, that works fine. You choose what you're comfortable with.

All interview data is deleted within 90 days of delivering your Opportunity Map. You can request deletion at any time. We are ICO registered and comply with UK GDPR. A full data handling pack is provided before the engagement starts.

Is This for You?

Built for owner-managed businesses

The AI Audit is designed for businesses with 10 to 150 people, where the owner wants to understand what AI could genuinely do for their specific operation, without wading through generic advice or committing to a large project upfront.

Professional services businesses get the most from it: law firms, accountancy practices, recruitment agencies, creative and marketing agencies. If your business runs on processes that repeat regularly, there are almost certainly opportunities waiting to be found.

This is not for you if:

  • You're already well down the road with AI and need technical delivery
  • You're looking for a big consultancy with a team of analysts
  • You want a quick framework to fill in yourself

Common questions

"We're too small for this kind of thing"

The audit is designed specifically for businesses with 10-150 people. This is the size where AI makes the biggest difference, and where the big consultancies don't bother looking. If anything, you're exactly the right size.

"We're already using some AI tools"

Good. That means you're not starting from zero. The audit will tell you whether you're using the right tools in the right places, and surface opportunities you haven't found yet. Tools without a map is the problem the audit solves.

"What do we actually get at the end?"

An interactive Opportunity Map with every AI and automation opportunity in your business, plotted on an impact-vs-effort matrix. Plus a PDF export and a 60-minute walkthrough session. It's a deliverable you can hand to your team, your board, or a developer and say: "This is the plan."

"I'd need to involve my team. Is that disruptive?"

Each team member gives one conversation, under an hour. No workshops, no away days, no homework. As the person engaging us, your total time commitment is around 2-3 hours across the whole engagement: a discovery call, a kickoff, and the walkthrough at the end. The interview approach brings the team in naturally. It's collaborative, not top-down.

"What about client confidentiality and data?"

We never access your systems, databases, or client data. The entire audit is interview-based. Recording is optional and flexible. All data is deleted within 90 days of delivery. We're ICO registered and provide a full data handling pack before the engagement starts.

"Can't I just figure this out myself?"

You could. Our free AI Mini-Map tool can help you start. The full audit goes deeper: independent interviews with your team surface things that don't come up when the owner is in the room, and the AI-assisted analysis catches patterns across conversations that self-assessment misses.
