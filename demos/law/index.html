Executive summary

What we found

Insights from our interviews with partners, the practice manager, and frontline fee earners across all four practice areas.

  1. Non-billable admin is the single largest capacity drain. Fee earners lose an estimated 12+ hours per week to document searching, formatting, and client chasing, consistent with Clio's finding that UK lawyers lose 2.3 hours per day to non-billable work.
  2. The firm has no document management system or structured precedent bank. Documents live in inconsistent SharePoint folders with no naming convention, leading to hours spent searching and multiple conflicting versions of standard templates.
  3. Lock-up stands at approximately 140 days (nearly five months of fee income tied up), driven by delayed billing on property completions and slow collection. Write-offs consume 10-15% of recorded time before it ever reaches an invoice.
  4. AML/CDD onboarding takes approximately 2 hours per new matter and periodic re-checks are materially behind schedule, against a backdrop where the SRA found only 13% of firms fully compliant in 2024-25 inspections.
  5. Staff are using ChatGPT with client data without any firm governance, approved tools list, or data handling policy, while PII insurers have begun asking about AI governance on proposal forms and the SRA published new AI guidance in February 2026.

North Star

"Free fee earners from low-value repetitive admin so they can focus on billable work, client relationships, and career development, while giving the firm the governance infrastructure to adopt AI safely and satisfy both the SRA and PII insurers."

— Whitfield Carr Solicitors, stated direction

Teams

Where the pain lives

Five practice areas, each with its own bottlenecks.

"Document drafting from precedents takes an hour per document: twenty minutes finding, forty-five minutes adapting, fifteen minutes of actual legal thinking."

— Heard across multiple practice-area interviews

Commercial Property & Conveyancing

  • Fee earners spending approximately one-third of time on non-billable admin: searching for precedents, reformatting lease clauses, chasing clients for documents
  • Document drafting from precedents takes ~1 hour per document: 20 minutes finding, 45 minutes adapting, 15 minutes actual legal thinking
  • Search result summaries for reports on title are manually written every time: same categories, same structure, same phrasing, repeated for every transaction
  • Lock-up at ~140 days driven by billing on completion and slow collection post-invoice

Corporate & Commercial

  • Document drafting inefficiency shared with property team: precedent searching in chaotic SharePoint folders
  • Institutional knowledge lost when staff leave: associate departure resulted in inability to find half the corporate documents she had been using
  • No CRM or pipeline tracking: client relationships and referral networks exist only in partners' heads and Outlook contacts

Private Client (Wills, Probate, Tax Planning)

  • No structured precedent bank despite 70% of wills following approximately six standard structures: solicitors rebuild from scratch or hunt through personal folders
  • Two hours spent searching for an LPA template saved in a subfolder with a filename that didn't mention LPA anywhere
  • AML/CDD onboarding burden shared with operations: private client work requires full CDD on every new matter

Employment & HR Advisory

  • Client communication overhead: routine status updates consume fee earner time that could be billable
  • Time recording inconsistency shared firm-wide: entries backfilled from memory, unreliable utilisation data

Practice Management & Administration

  • Weekly WIP reporting requires 3-4 hour manual LEAP-to-Excel process every Monday: no self-serve dashboard for partners
  • Time recording data unreliable: entries backfilled, one associate submitted 11 hours for a single Tuesday
  • Write-offs of 10-15% of recorded time: slow billing erodes recovery rate, estimated £310k+ at risk annually
  • AML/CDD onboarding takes approximately 2 hours per new matter with periodic re-checks materially behind schedule: only 13% of firms fully compliant per SRA
  • No document management system: inconsistent SharePoint folders, no naming convention, multiple conflicting versions of standard documents
  • COLP/COFA compliance burden falls on one partner alongside a full caseload: no time to develop AI governance policy despite SRA and insurer expectations
  • PII insurers now asking about AI governance on proposal forms: first flagged by broker this renewal cycle
